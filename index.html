Accessibility Conformance Checklist

Ensuring digital accessibility is essential for making technology usable by everyone, including people with disabilities. This Accessibility Evaluation Checklist is designed to help you assess whether your web applications and digital content meet key accessibility standards.

Each criterion in this checklist covers an important aspect of accessible design, from visual and keyboard navigation to contrast, color use, and support for assistive technologies. As you go through the checklist, you’ll be guided step-by-step through a series of requirements based on established guidelines such as the W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) and platform accessibility best practices.

For each item, review your content or application and mark whether it passes or fails the given criterion. At the end, you’ll receive a summary score and can download your results for documentation or further review.

By following this checklist, you can identify potential barriers and ensure your products are more inclusive, usable, and compliant with accessibility standards.

Alien Instruments UBIK: The Talking MIDI Mapper

Welcome to Alien Instruments UBIK, the talking MIDI mapping solution. Create groups for your controls and start adding sliders and buttons to send MIDI CC’s. Completely accessible for screen reading and keyboard navigation. Labelling groups and components also labels for screen reading. Design your own interface and map it to the device or software you wish to control. Each slider has a MIDI learn button, click this and follow the voice prompts to map your controller. When adding a slider, you can map the output range displayed on the screen and spoken aloud to and integer or float to match the parameter you are controlling. Alternatively, you can display or hear the current MIDI CC value. For buttons you can set the on value to any cc value, when mapped this will speak as “parameter name” on and off and display the same in the button. To get started click the add group button and follow the prompts. Or take a guided tour of the interface and settings by clicking the "Start Tour" button.

If you are using a screen reader to take the tour after clicking start tour focus will go to a heading level 2, this is the parameter name, and that component shows focus in the UI. Use the tab key and next will be a heading level 3 with a description and context of the area or item. Press tab again and you will focus on the next button, followed by previous and then close buttons. When the tour ends there will be a finish button.

Have fun and enjoy!

Alien Instruments Atom Synth

Particle Synth is an interactive, browser-based audio-visual synthesizer that fuses real-time 3D particle physics with polyphonic sound synthesis, modulation, and MIDI control. At its core, Particle Synth uses a physics engine (Cannon.js) and 3D rendering (Three.js) to simulate and display electrons, protons, and neutrons in a bounded virtual space. Each particle’s movement and position can be mapped as modulation sources to sound parameters of a versatile dual-oscillator polyphonic synth, built with Tone.js.

Users can:

Spawn and control particles (electrons, protons, neutrons), each with unique physics behaviors and customizable visual appearance.

Assign particle axes (X, Y, Z) as modulation sources for a wide range of synth and effect parameters—essentially turning atomic movement into dynamic audio modulation.

Shape sounds with a flexible dual-oscillator architecture, multimode filter, envelope controls, portamento, and a chain of effects (delay, tremolo, auto-filter, phaser, pitch shift, and more).

Automate and randomize notes, modulate sound using real physics, and save or recall complete sound setups as presets.

Map MIDI CCs to any parameter using a built-in MIDI learn system.

Visualize all particle activity and sound shaping in real-time in a 3D environment, complete with interactive UI panels for both sound and physics parameters.

The result is a unique “atomic synthesizer” where sound and visuals are intrinsically connected: physical particle dynamics become modulation sources, allowing users to experiment with sound design in an intuitive, playful, and deeply interactive way.

Alien Instruments SpectraSynth

SpectraSynth is an advanced web-based synthesizer and modular effects workstation powered by the Web Audio API. At its heart is a truly expressive 2D morphing oscillator—instantly blend between saw, square, triangle, and custom harmonic waveforms using an intuitive XY pad or sliders for seamless, real-time timbral exploration.

Main Features:

Accessible, Customizable Interface: Navigate fully by keyboard. Screen-reader friendly with ARIA live regions. Supports alternate color themes, gradients, and multiple knob styles for a personalized experience.

2D Morphing Oscillator: Morph continuously between classic and user-defined waveforms. Shape and sculpt unique timbres visually via XY pad, precise sliders, or MIDI.

Deep Modulation: Two independent LFOs and dual envelopes (amp and mod) animate your sound. Modulate filter, pitch, amplitude, or even the morph position itself for evolving, animated tones. LFOs and envelopes are freely assignable.

Playability: Perform with your QWERTY keyboard (musical typing) or any MIDI controller. Enjoy full pitch bend and velocity support for expressive play.

Modular Drag-and-Drop Effects: Design your dream FX chain: drag in chorus, phaser, delay, distortion, mutators, morphing filters, EQ, compressor, ring mod, auto-panner, and more. Rearrange, tweak, or remove effects at any time with instant feedback.

MIDI Learn Everywhere: Right-click or press "M" to instantly map it to a MIDI knob, slider, or button. Save, export, and import your MIDI CC mappings for total control and flexibility.

Presets & State Management: Save and recall both synth and FX chain presets. Export/import all your sounds and setups as portable JSON files for easy backup or sharing.

Visual Feedback: See a live harmonic spectrum and real-time stereo level meter so you can monitor and refine your sound visually as well as by ear.

SpectraSynth is designed for sound designers, synth enthusiasts, and performers who want deep, hands-on sound creation in the browser. No installs, no limits—just open and play.

Alien Instruments Phonetical

Phonetical is a real-time, multi-accent, programmable speech synthesizer that lets you create and shape speech, syllables, and singing—one phoneme at a time. Instead of stringing together audio samples or relying on black-box TTS, Phonetical synthesizes the sound of human (and inhuman!) speech directly from its acoustic building blocks: vowels and consonants, constructed using formant synthesis and bandpass filtering.

Key Features:

True Phoneme Synthesis: Every sound is synthesized from scratch—no pre-recorded samples, just pure oscillators, noise, and filters.

Multi-Accent: Choose from multiple built-in accent presets, including American and British English, Spanish, French, German, Japanese, Hindi, Mandarin, and Italian.

Flexible Input: Type words, phoneme sequences (IPA or ARPAbet), or even play speech live via your MIDI keyboard.

Expressive Speech: Fine-tune vibrato, formant morphing, consonant boost, and more to shape robotic or naturalistic voices.

Realtime Control: All parameters respond instantly, and speech can be triggered chromatically—great for talking synth leads, vocal effects, or experimental sound design.

MIDI Learn & Presets: Map any parameter to MIDI controls, save and recall custom settings, or export/import your patches.

Modular Drag-and-Drop Effects: Design your dream FX chain: drag in chorus, phaser, delay, distortion, mutators, morphing filters, EQ, compressor, ring mod, auto-panner, and more. Rearrange, tweak, or remove effects at any time with instant feedback.

Unlike typical speech engines, Phonetical is designed for creative exploration: It’s a tool for musicians, sound designers, linguists, and tinkerers who want hands-on control over the mechanics of speech synthesis. Play with formants, accent switching, live vowel morphing, or MIDI-driven talking basslines. See how “robotic” or “natural” you can make your synthetic voice.

Alien Instruments Morpheus

This project is a feature-rich, browser-based wavetable synthesizer designed for exploration, accessibility, and creativity. Built with the Web Audio API and modern JavaScript, it offers a responsive, fully customizable synth engine with an intuitive, modular UI—no installation required.

Key Features

Wavetable Synthesis: Load and blend between diverse user-provided wavetables—Bass, FM, Granular, Distorted, and more—giving you a vast sonic palette for sound design.

Advanced Modulation Matrix: Modulate any parameter by connecting LFO sources to almost any control. Accessible Drag & Drop Modulation: Assign modulation using both mouse and keyboard. Mouse: Drag LFO "chips" onto any parameter slider. Keyboard: Use P key to pick up an LFO source and drop it on any focused control, with ARIA live feedback for screen reader users.

Multi-Effect Chain: Includes dual and morphing filters, delay, distortion, ring modulation, and convolution reverb, all tweakable from the UI.

Preset Management: Save, load, import, and export your patches and MIDI mappings—presets persist in your browser, and can be shared or backed up as JSON.

Built-in Keyboard & MIDI Support: Play notes with your typing keyboard or any connected MIDI controller.

Accessibility-First Design

Screen Reader Announcements: Key UI actions (like assigning modulation) are announced via a live ARIA region for non-visual users.

Keyboard Navigable: Every slider, button, and modulation target is focusable and operable without a mouse.

Designed for Sound Designers & Explorers

Whether you want to craft unique textures, experiment with modulation, or personalize your synth’s appearance, this project provides a playground that’s both powerful and welcoming. All signal routing and modulation are visually clear, with animated modulation rings, real-time UI color theming, and live feedback for every tweak.
