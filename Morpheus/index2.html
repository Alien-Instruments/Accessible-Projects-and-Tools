Synth Manual

Overview

Morpheus is a feature-rich, browser-based wavetable synthesizer built for sonic exploration, accessibility, and creativityright in your web browser, with no installation required.

Morpheus puts you in control with powerful wavetable synthesis, a flexible modulation matrix, a full suite of effects, and a modern modular UI. Every aspect of the synth can be controlled by voice commands, mouse, keyboard, or MIDImaking it truly hands-free and accessible to everyone.

Key Features

  • Wavetable Synthesis: Load and blend between a wide range of wavetablesincluding Bass, FM, Granular, and Distorted shapesfor a huge variety of sounds.
  • Drag & Drop Modulation: Assign modulation by dragging LFO "chips" onto any control.
  • Keyboard Modulation: Assign modulation targets using just the keyboard, with ARIA live feedback for screen readers.
  • MIDI Learn: Instantly map any MIDI controller to any synth parameter.
  • Multi-Effect Chain: Shape your sound with dual morphing filters, delay, distortion, ring modulation, and convolution reverball easily tweakable from the UI.
  • Preset Management: Save, load, import, and export patches and MIDI mappings as JSON. All your presets are stored in your browser and can be easily shared or backed up.
  • Performance-Ready Controls: Play Morpheus with your computer keyboard or any MIDI device. Every control is fully accessible and screen-reader friendly.
  • Accessibility-First Design: All major actions are announced via ARIA live regions, and the interface is designed for use with mouse, keyboard, MIDI, and voice.
  • Voice Control: Change any parameter, assign modulation, or perform drag-and-drop actions using simple spoken commandsno hands required.

Designed for Creators

Morpheus is built for anyone who wants to experiment, customize, and create. Signal flow and modulation are visually clear, with animated feedback for every tweak. Real-time color theming and live announcements make the experience welcoming and inspiring for all users.

Whether you're shaping classic analog sounds, designing cinematic textures, or exploring uncharted sonic territory, Morpheus is your playground for creative synthesisnow with complete voice control.

Voice Control

Morpheus lets you control every parameter and modulation using your voice.
Click the Voice button (or press V), then clearly say a command.

What Can I Say?

  • Set Any Parameter:
    set [group] [parameter] [value]
    [group] [parameter] [value]
    Examples:
    set amp envelope attack 0.4
    oscillator a detune -400
    filter a drive 40
    delay feedback 0.5
  • Increment or Decrement a Parameter:
    [group] [parameter] up [amount] or down [amount]
    Examples:
    Reverb wet up 0.1,
    Oscillator A detune down 200
  • Change a Dropdown (select option):
    set [group] [parameter] [option]
    [group] [parameter] [option]
    change [group] [parameter] [option]
    select [group] [parameter] [option]
    Examples:
    oscillator a wave a Bass 3
    filter a type a high
    lfo 2 shape triangle
    reverb decay medium
  • Turn On/Off Toggles:
    [group] [parameter] on, [group] [parameter] off
    enable [group] [parameter], disable [group] [parameter]
    turn [group] [parameter] on/off
    Examples:
    filter morph bypass on
    distortion bypass off
    dual filter routing serial
    delay bypass off
  • Focus a Section or Control:
    focus [group] [parameter]
    go to [group] [parameter]
    select [group] [parameter]
    Examples:
    focus amp envelope attack
    go to oscillator a detune
    select filter a frequency a
  • Query Ranges or Options:
    [group] [parameter] range
    [group] [parameter] options
    [group] [parameter] choices
    [group] [parameter] values
    Examples:
    oscillator b detune range
    amp envelope sustain range
    lfo 2 shape options
    filter b type b choices
  • Modulation by Voice (LFO or Envelope assignment):
    Pick Up: pick up lfo 1, pick up mod envelope 2, grab lfo 3, grab mod envelope 1
    Drop: drop on [group] [parameter]
    Examples:
    pick up lfo 1 → drop on filter a frequency a
    pick up mod envelope 2 → drop on amp envelope attack
  • Remove Modulation:
    remove [lfo|envelope|modulation] from [group] [parameter]
    clear [lfo|envelope|modulation] from [group] [parameter]
    delete [lfo|envelope|modulation] from [group] [parameter]
    remove modulation from [group] [parameter]
    Examples:
    remove lfo from filter a frequency a
    remove envelope from amp envelope decay
    remove modulation from oscillator b detune
    clear lfo 2 from morph
  • MIDI Learn:
    midi learn [group] [parameter]
    cancel midi learn
    Examples:
    midi learn filter a frequency a
    midi learn amp envelope attack
    cancel midi learn
  • Announcement Mode:
    Press Shift+A to toggle spoken or screen reader announcements
  • Start Voice Control:
    Press V key or click the Voice button

Parameter Names

Always use the group/module name as shown below:

  • Oscillator A: wave a, wave b, detune, volume
  • Oscillator B: wave a, wave b, detune, volume
  • Oscillator Mod: lfo rate, lfo depth, lfo target, lfo shape, morph, phase mod
  • Filter A: drive, type a, frequency a, resonance a, type b, frequency b, resonance b, routing
  • Filter B (Morphing Filter): type a, frequency a, resonance a, type b, frequency b, resonance b, morph, rate, depth
  • Amp Envelope: attack, decay, sustain, release
  • Mod Envelope 1: attack, decay, sustain, release
  • Mod Envelope 2: attack, decay, sustain, release
  • LFO 1/2/3: rate, shape
  • Delay: bypass, time, feedback, cut off, wet
  • Distortion: bypass, drive, curve
  • Ring Mod: bypass, freq, depth
  • Reverb: bypass, decay, duration, high cut, low cut, wet

Tips

  • Use parameter names as they appear above, always with their group/module.
  • Say one command at a time and wait for a response.
  • For dropdowns, say the value as it appears in the UI.
  • For modulation, always "pick up" and then "drop on".
  • For toggles, say "on/off", "enable/disable", or (where supported) "serial/parallel".

Oscillators A & B

Morpheus features two powerful wavetable oscillatorsOscillator A and Oscillator Bthat form the core of your sound. Each oscillator loads two waveforms (Wave A and Wave B), and the Morph control blends smoothly between them for evolving or hybrid tones.

  • Morph: Crossfades between Wave A and Wave B. Full left = Wave A, full right = Wave B, or set anywhere between. Morph can also be modulated by the LFO for animated movement.
  • Detune: Fine-tunes each oscillators pitch (in cents). Slight detuning creates wider, richer sounds.
  • Volume: Adjusts the level of each oscillator in the mix.

Phase Modulation (FM):
Oscillator B can modulate the phase of Oscillator A using the Mod Amount control, adding metallic or bell-like digital tones for unique, complex sounds.

Custom Wavetables:
Load any compatible wavetables into Oscillators A and B. Each oscillator morphs between its Wave A and Wave B for endless sonic variety.

Tips:
  • Slightly detune A and B, or layer different waves for thick, wide sounds.
  • Modulate Morph with the LFO for dynamic motion.
  • Increase Mod Amount for FM-style, metallic, or evolving tones.

Use Oscillators A & B alone, layered, or with phase modulation for everything from classic analog to cutting-edge digital sounds.

Filters

Morpheus gives you two versatile filter typesDual Filter and Morphing Filterdesigned for both classic tone-shaping and modern, animated filtering effects.

Dual Filter

The Dual Filter lets you sculpt your sound with two separate filters (A & B) that can be run in series (one after the other) or parallel (side-by-side). Each filter has independent type and frequency controls, plus a soft overdrive stage for added character.

  • Filter Types: Each filter can be set to low-pass, high-pass, or other types for flexible sound shaping.
  • Drive: Adds gentle distortion for warmth and edge.
  • Serial/Parallel Routing: Switch between classic serial filtering or wide, parallel tones.
  • Freq A / Freq B: Sets the cutoff frequency for each filter.

Morphing Filter

The Morphing Filter blends between two filter types (e.g., low-pass and high-pass) using a single Morph control. This lets you sweep smoothly between very different filter shapes, creating rich, animated timbres.

  • Filter A & Filter B: Each has its own type, cutoff, and resonance settings.
  • Morph: Crossfades between Filter A and Filter B for dynamic filter motion.
  • Output Gain: Controls the output level of the filter block.
  • LFO Modulation: Morph can be modulated at audio-rate by its own built-in LFO for movement and animation.

Filter Modulation Controls

  • Bypass: Turns filter LFO modulation on or off.
  • LFO Rate: Controls how quickly the filter morphs, from very slow sweeps to fast modulation (0.01 to 5 Hz).
  • LFO Depth: Adjusts how much the LFO affects the filter morph amount.
  • Filter Mix: Sets the balance between Filter A and Filter B outputs (0 = all A, 1 = all B, 0.5 = even blend).
Tips:
  • Use Serial mode for traditional filter sweeps, Parallel for broader, layered textures.
  • Automate Morph with the LFO for rhythmic or evolving filter effects.
  • Increase Drive for extra bite, especially on bass or leads.
  • Combine with modulation matrix for even more creative filter movement.

Envelopes

Morpheus provides three powerful envelopes to shape your sound:

  • Amp Envelope (ADSR):
    Controls the volume contour for every note. Classic Attack, Decay, Sustain, and Release settings let you craft punchy plucks, smooth pads, or long evolving fades.
    • Attack: Time to reach full volume when a note is pressed.
    • Decay: Time to fall from full volume to sustain level.
    • Sustain: Level held while you hold the note.
    • Release: Time to fade out after the note is released.
  • Modulation Envelopes (2x ADSR):
    Two additional envelopes can be routed to modulate any parameter you choosesuch as filter cutoff, pitch, oscillator morph, FX mix, or even LFO rate/depth. This allows deep, evolving and expressive sound design.
    • Assign these envelopes to any destination using the modulation matrix, drag & drop, keyboard, or voice control.
    • Each modulation envelope works independently and can have its own timing and shape.

Creative Hints:

  • Use the amp envelope for shaping note dynamicsshort attack and release for percussive sounds, long attack and release for lush pads.
  • Route a mod envelope to filter cutoff for classic synth sweeps, or to pitch for wild laser/zap FX.
  • Combine envelopes with LFOs for even more complex, moving modulation.

LFOs & Modulation

Morpheus features flexible LFOs (Low Frequency Oscillators) that let you modulate almost any parameterbringing your sounds to life with motion and expression.

  • Modulate Anything (Mouse): Drag an LFO output ring and drop it onto any slider to assign modulation.
    • Once dropped, drag the target slider to set the modulation depth (how much the LFO moves that parameter).
    • To remove modulation from a control, hold Shift and left-click the slider.
  • Keyboard Modulation:
    • Press P to pick up the focused LFO source.
    • Navigate to your target slider and press P again to drop and assign modulation.
    • Press Shift+P to remove modulation from the focused control.
  • Voice Control:
    • Say Pick up LFO one (or two/three), then Drop on [parameter] to assign modulation by voice.
    • Say Remove modulation from [parameter] or use Shift-P as above.

Tip: Use LFOs to modulate filter cutoff, oscillator morph, effects, or any creative parameter for evolving and expressive patches!

Delay

  • Bypass: Turns the delay effect on or off instantly. Use this to compare your sound with and without echo.
  • Time: Sets how long it takes for the echo to repeat, from fast slapback (20ms) to long, spacious repeats (up to 1000ms). Try short times for a "doubling" effect or long times for spacious, rhythmic echoes.
  • Feedback: Controls how much of the delayed signal is fed back into the effect. Low feedback gives a single repeat, while high feedback creates multiple echoes or even self-oscillating effects. Experiment with high feedback for dreamy ambient tails or special effects.
  • Cutoff: Applies a lowpass filter to the repeated echoes, making each successive repeat sound darker and less bright. This mimics classic tape delays and keeps echoes from cluttering the mix.
  • Wet: Adjusts the blend between your dry (original) signal and the delayed echoes. Set it low for subtle space, or high for a fully echoed effect.

Creative tip: Automate delay time or feedback with modulation or MIDI to create dub-style delay throws, rhythmic stutters, or pitch-shifting echo effects.

Distortion

  • Bypass: Turns distortion on or off for instant A/B comparison.
  • Drive: Sets how much your signal is boosted and distorted. Low drive adds gentle warmth and analog character; high drive introduces crunch, grit, or even fuzz.
  • Curve: Changes the shape and intensity of the distortion. Try subtle curves for smooth saturation or extreme settings for aggressive, digital distortion.

Creative tip: Use distortion to make basses cut through the mix, add edge to leads, or transform simple waveforms into aggressive, modern tones.

Ring Modulation

  • Bypass: Switches ring modulation on or off.
  • Freq: Sets the frequency of the modulator signal. Low frequencies (below 20Hz) produce tremolo-like effects; higher frequencies (above 50Hz) create metallic, bell-like, or robotic textures.
  • Depth: Controls how much the ring modulator affects the sound. Lower depth blends in the effect subtly, while higher depth produces more dramatic, experimental results.

Creative tip: Animate ring mod frequency with an LFO for evolving timbres, or try modulating depth for rhythmic movement. Great for sci-fi sound design or complex percussive tones.

Reverb

  • Bypass: Instantly toggles the reverb effect.
  • Decay / Duration: Sets how long the reverb tail lasts, from a tight, boxy room (Shortest) to endless, atmospheric washes (Longest). Choose a short decay for snappy percussion, or long reverb for ambient pads and drones.
  • High Cut / Low Cut: Filters the frequencies of the reverb, letting you make the effect darker (high cut), brighter, or filtered for vintage and modern textures.
  • Wet: Sets how much reverb is mixed with your dry sound. Subtle settings can add depth and space; 100% wet can create surreal, fully-ambient sounds.

Creative tip: Automate wet level or decay to create build-ups, breakdowns, or transitions. Filtering the reverb tail with high/low cut can help vocals or leads sit perfectly in a busy mix.

Preset Manager

Morpheus includes a powerful Preset Manager so you can save, recall, share, and organize your favorite synth sounds and configurations with ease.

  • Save: Store all current synth settingsincluding sliders, toggles, menu choices, and modulation assignmentsunder a custom name for easy recall.
  • Load: Instantly restore all parameters and routings from any saved preset with a single click.
  • Delete: Remove presets you no longer need to keep your library organized.
  • Export: Download all your presets as a portable .json file for backup or sharing.
  • Import: Load presets from other users or past sessions by importing .json files. Supports both single and multiple preset imports.

Accessibility: All preset actions are accessible by mouse or keyboard. ARIA live feedback announces each action for screen readers. Presets are stored locally for quick accessno cloud required.

Editing Shortcuts

  1. Open the Edit Shortcuts panel.
  2. Click the Change button for the shortcut you want to set.
  3. Press the new key combination (modifiers first, then the main key).
  4. Your shortcut is updated and saved automatically.

Tip: If you set a shortcut that's already assigned, you'll be notified to pick a different combination. All shortcuts are accessible and announced for screen reader users.
